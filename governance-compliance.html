Compliance, Governance & Risk

Strengthen your security posture with policies, controls, and frameworks that reduce risk.

Overview

Strong cybersecurity isn’t just about tools — it’s about having the right policies, governance, and risk management practices in place. We help organizations build structured, repeatable security programs aligned with industry standards and regulatory requirements.

Whether you’re preparing for an audit, improving your security maturity, or building a governance framework from scratch, we provide the expertise and structure to guide your program.

What We Support

  • Security policies and governance frameworks
  • Risk assessments and mitigation planning
  • Compliance readiness and gap analysis
  • Data protection and privacy controls
  • Vendor and third‑party risk management
  • Security awareness and training programs

Governance & Compliance Capabilities

  • Policy Development: Create or update security policies and standards.
  • Risk Management: Identify, score, and prioritize risks.
  • Framework Alignment: Map controls to NIST, CIS, ISO, SOC 2, or HIPAA.
  • Audit Preparation: Support for internal and external audits.
  • Data Governance: Classify, protect, and manage sensitive data.
  • Training & Awareness: Build a security‑first culture.

Typical Engagements

  • Security program development and maturity assessments
  • Compliance readiness for SOC 2, HIPAA, ISO 27001, or NIST CSF
  • Risk assessments and remediation planning
  • Creation of security policies, standards, and procedures
  • Vendor risk management program design

Our Approach

  1. Assess: Review current policies, risks, and compliance requirements.
  2. Define: Establish governance structure and security objectives.
  3. Implement: Deploy policies, controls, and risk management processes.
  4. Align: Map controls to frameworks and regulatory requirements.
  5. Improve: Build long‑term maturity and continuous improvement cycles.

Benefits

  • Clearer understanding of organizational risk
  • Stronger alignment with compliance frameworks
  • More consistent and repeatable security practices
  • Reduced audit findings and regulatory exposure
  • Improved security culture and accountability
